## Analysis

The **Prelude in C Major** (BWV 846) is one of the most famous keyboard works by [Johann Sebastian Bach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach). It serves as the opening movement of _The Well-Tempered Clavier, Book I_.

### Structure

The piece is built almost entirely on arpeggiated chords.

**Ex. 1: Theme (Arpeggio Pattern)**

```abc

M:4/4
L:1/16
K:C
%%score_system_on
z2 E2 G2 c2 e2 G2 c2 e2 | z2 F2 A2 d2 f2 A2 d2 f2 |
```

It explores various harmonic progressions before arriving at the final C Major chord.

### Ending

The piece concludes with a series of C Major chords, resolving all tension.
